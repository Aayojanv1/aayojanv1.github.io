The story

A kitchen built on flavour memory

"

Munia's Kitchen begins with one belief — every plate should carry a memory. The mustard cling of a Doi Ilish from a monsoon afternoon. The slow-cooked patience of a Galouti kebab. The crunch of a Crab Wonton stolen off a Diwali platter.

Karabi Dey has spent years perfecting that bridge — from grandmother's Bengali rice plates to crisp, modern continental finishes. What lands at your event is not a menu. It is a moment.

Signature dishes

The plates that define Munia's Kitchen

Five recipes the kitchen is known for. Each one is a request guests come back for.

🐟 Bengali · Signature

Doi Ilish

Hilsa cooked in a delicate yoghurt-mustard gravy. The cornerstone of a Bengali feast — luminous, fragrant, unforgettable.

🍢 Mughlai · Slow craft

Mutton Galouti Kebab

Minced mutton bound only with smoke and spice. Melts on the tongue — the dish that earned its name from "Galaawat" itself.

🍛 Mughlai · Crowd favourite

Mutton Biryani

Long-grain rice layered with hand-trimmed mutton, slow-dum cooked. Aromatic, never greasy. The plate guests photograph first.

🥟 Chinese · Live counter

Crab Wonton

Hand-folded wontons, crab and water-chestnut filling, fried crisp. A live-counter winner — gone within minutes of being served.

🌿 Bengali · Heirloom

Kochu Pata Bhapa Chingri

Prawns steamed inside colocasia leaves with coconut and mustard. A heritage Bengali preparation few caterers still attempt.
